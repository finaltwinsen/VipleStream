This app streams games, programs, or your full desktop from your PC running VipleStream-Server (or upstream <a href="https://github.com/LizardByte/Sunshine">Sunshine</a>) on your local network or the Internet. Mouse, keyboard, and controller input is sent from your Android device to the PC. VipleStream is a fork of Moonlight with built-in NAT traversal (relay), native frame interpolation (FRUC), and performance optimizations.

Streaming performance may vary based on your client device and network setup. HDR requires an HDR10-capable device, a GPU that can encode HEVC Main 10, and an HDR10-enabled game.

'''Features'''
* Open-source and completely free (no ads, IAPs, or "Pro")
* Streams games purchased from any store
* Works on your home network, over the Internet, over LTE, or through the built-in relay server
* Built-in frame-rate up-conversion (FRUC) for smoother streaming on slower networks
* Up to 4K 120 FPS HDR streaming with 7.1 surround sound
* H.264, HEVC, and AV1 codec support (AV1 requires VipleStream-Server / Sunshine and a supported host GPU)
* Keyboard and mouse support (best with Android 8.0 or later)
* Stylus/S-Pen support
* Supports PS3/4/5, Xbox 360/One/Series, and Android gamepads
* Local co-op with up to 16 connected controllers (max of 4 controllers with GFE)
* Force feedback and gamepad motion sensor support
* Mouse control via gamepad by long-pressing Start

'''Quick Setup Host Instructions for VipleStream-Server (all GPUs)'''
# Install VipleStream-Server on your PC from https://github.com/finaltwinsen/VipleStream/releases
# Navigate to the VipleStream-Server Web UI on your PC for first-time setup
# Tap on the PC in VipleStream and type the PIN in the VipleStream-Server Web UI on your PC
# Start streaming!

'''Quick Setup Host Instructions for GeForce Experience (NVIDIA-only, legacy)'''
# Make sure GeForce Experience is open on your PC. Turn on GameStream in the SHIELD settings page.
# Tap on the PC in VipleStream and type the PIN on your PC
# Start streaming!

To have a good experience, you need a mid to high-end wireless router with a good wireless connection to your Android device (5 GHz highly recommended) and a good connection from your PC to your router (Ethernet highly recommended).
